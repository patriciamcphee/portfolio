This cheat sheet serves as a quick reference for efficiently creating, reviewing, and maintaining high-quality DevOps documentation with the support of a Technical Content Manager.



## Planning 

- **Document Type:** Internal or external?
- **User Needs:** What does the user need to know and do? 
  :::tip 
  This aligns with their goal or outcome of a scenario.
  :::
- **Content Reuse:** Is the content duplicated elsewhere?
- **Collaborate:** Work with others to avoid duplication.
- **Media:** Should you include videos or GIFs?

## Creation 

- **Technical Accuracy:** Are all facts and terms correct? 
- **Content Structure:** Is the content logically structured? 
- **Complexity:** Simplify complex concepts where needed.
- **Hierarchy:** Keep to a maximum of four levels.
- **Headings:** Avoid redundant headings.

## Review & Approval 

- **Reviewers Identified:** One for accuracy, one for readability.
- **Clear Deadlines:** Set a deadline for feedback.
- **Significant Changes:** Consider another review if needed.
- **Feedback Incorporated:** Have you made the necessary changes?

## Publishing 

- **Final Proofread:** Check for any errors.
- **Media Check:** Are images and diagrams correctly placed?
- **Access Permissions:** Ensure the right audience can see it.
- **Communicate:** Notify stakeholders once published.

## Maintenance

- **Feedback Collection:** Set up a feedback method.
- **Regular Reviews:** Monthly or quarterly content audits.
- **Content Archiving:** Remove or update outdated content.
