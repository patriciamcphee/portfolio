Creating engaging and user-friendly content extends beyond just the words you write; it also involves how you integrate hyperlinks. They should be informative and woven into your narrative, guiding readers to additional valuable resources.

## Basic guidelines

- **Be Descriptive**: Hyperlink text should accurately describe the linked content to provide users with clear expectations of where the link will take them.
- **Avoid Generic Text**: Instead of using generic phrases like "click here" or "read more," use descriptive text that conveys the purpose or destination of the link.
- **Use Keywords**: Incorporate relevant keywords in the hyperlink text to improve search engine optimization (SEO) and help users quickly identify the linked topic.
- **Keep it Concise**: Aim for brevity in hyperlink text to maintain a clean and uncluttered appearance. Ideally, keep the text short while conveying the essential information.

## Links to third-party documentation

In addition to the basic guidelines mentioned, avoid putting quotation marks around the link and ensure you use the exact title of the third-party document.
