AI-generated content offers exciting opportunities to streamline workflows and deliver high-quality content efficiently. While AI tools enhance productivity and creativity, using them effectively requires careful planning. 

By following these guidelines and addressing issues (observed through recent content reviews), you can significantly enhance the quality and effectiveness of your technical documentation.

## Guidelines for using AI-generated content

When incorporating AI-generated content into technical documentation, maintaining high standards of quality and accuracy is essential.

This means:

- **Thorough review and adherence to standards:** Treat AI-generated content as a starting point, not the final product. 
  - Check for duplicate content, as multiple sections may inadvertently cover the same topic, leading to confusion and redundancy.
  - Ensure content follows your style guide and accurately conveys technical concepts. Use title case for titles and sentence case for headers to maintain consistent, professional documentation.
  - Ensure it is detailed and comprehensive. Avoid using simple lists and bullet points without proper explanations and examples to guide readers through the product or process.
  - Structure bullet lists for easy scanning. Keep points direct and concise.
- **Complement, don’t replace:** Use AI to augment human expertise, not to replace it. Leverage AI tools for efficiency but rely on human judgment for clarity, tone, and nuance.
- **Be transparent:** Disclose when AI tools contribute to your content. Transparency fosters trust and helps users understand how the content was created.



## Best practices for leveraging AI in documentation

- **Automate repetitive tasks:** Use AI to handle routine, time-consuming tasks like generating boilerplate text or formatting. For instance, AI can automatically generate standard operating procedures (SOPs) by filling in templates with specific details. This allows human writers to concentrate on more complex and creative work.
- **Train on quality data:** Ensure your AI models learn from accurate, reliable, and inclusive datasets to produce high-quality content. 

  :::caution 
  Few AI models are currently trained on such high-quality datasets. With that said, always review the output to ensure it aligns with the style guide and accurately conveys technical concepts.
  :::

- **Stay current:** Regularly review and update AI-generated content to ensure it remains accurate, relevant, and aligned with evolving standards.
- **Monitor for bias:** Proactively check AI-generated content for biases and ethical concerns. Avoid gender stereotypes in technical examples, use inclusive language (such as "team members" rather than "guys"), and ensure documentation represents diverse scenarios and perspectives.


## Pitfalls of AI-generated content

AI can be a powerful tool, but understanding its limitations is crucial for creating meaningful, impactful, and ethical content. While AI tools have transformed content creation with unprecedented speed and efficiency, they present critical challenges that demand our attention:

- **Lack of style guide adherence:** AI tools don't inherently understand or follow organizational style guides, potentially creating inconsistencies in terminology, formatting, and voice.
- **Lack of contextual understanding:** AI struggles with subtle nuances and context, which can result in misinterpretations or content that misses the mark entirely.
- **Bias in the machine:** Because AI learns from existing data, it can unintentionally amplify biases in its training, creating content that may lack diversity or perpetuate stereotypes.
- **Ethical dilemmas:** AI raises questions about transparency and fairness—who gets credit for the content, and how does it impact opportunities for human creators?
- **Creativity in a box:** While efficient, AI tends to churn out formulaic and predictable content, often missing the originality, emotional depth, and creative spark that only humans can bring.
