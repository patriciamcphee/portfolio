## Overview

  <div className="rounded-lg border border-(--border) bg-(--card) overflow-hidden">
    <div className="px-5 py-3 bg-(--muted) border-b border-(--border)">
      <h3 className="text-base font-semibold text-(--foreground) m-0">What is it</h3>
    </div>
    <div className="px-5 py-4 text-sm text-(--foreground)">
      <span className="inline-block px-2 py-0.5 text-xs font-semibold rounded bg-(--primary) text-white mb-2">Required</span>
      <ul>
        <li>A single, high-level topic designed to introduce and explain a product, service, or tool from a technical perspective.</li>
        <li>Focuses on providing essential information, including its purpose, key features, capabilities, and how it fits within the larger system or environment.</li>
        <li>Sets the stage for deeper exploration.</li>
        <li>Avoids detailed step-by-step instructions, focusing instead on the &quot;big picture.&quot;</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  <div className="rounded-lg border border-(--border) bg-(--card) overflow-hidden">

    <div className="px-5 py-4 text-sm text-(--foreground)">

        <li>Orient readers before leading them into more specialized or technical documentation. For example, tutorials, API references, or configuration guides.</li>

## Concepts

<div className="not-prose grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-6 my-6">
  <div className="rounded-lg border border-(--border) bg-(--card) overflow-hidden">
    <div className="px-5 py-3 bg-(--muted) border-b border-(--border)">
      <h3 className="text-base font-semibold text-(--foreground) m-0">What is it</h3>
    </div>
    <div className="px-5 py-4 text-sm text-(--foreground)">
      <span className="inline-block px-2 py-0.5 text-xs font-semibold rounded bg-(--muted) text-(--muted-foreground) border border-(--border) mb-2">Recommended</span>
      <ul>
        <li>Centers on explaining the underlying principles, theories, or ideas behind a technology or methodology. It's more about the 'WHY' and conceptual understanding.</li>
        <li>Usually provides a high-level overview, explaining concepts and ideas without delving into technical specifics.</li>
        <li>Generally presented in a narrative format, discussing concepts in a logical, often sequential manner.</li>
        <li>More likely to be read once or occasionally to build foundational understanding.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-lg border border-(--border) bg-(--card) overflow-hidden">
    <div className="px-5 py-3 bg-(--muted) border-b border-(--border)">
      <h3 className="text-base font-semibold text-(--foreground) m-0">When to use it</h3>
    </div>
    <div className="px-5 py-4 text-sm text-(--foreground)">
      <ul>
        <li>When introducing a new technology or concept that requires foundational understanding before practical application.</li>
        <li>Before users dive into procedural guides or tutorials, to give them the necessary context.</li>
        <li>When explaining complex ideas that benefit from a higher-level overview before getting into specific technical details.</li>
        <li>When there&#39;s a need to clarify the reasoning behind design decisions, trade-offs, or technical choices.</li>
      </ul>
      <h5>EXAMPLES</h5>
      <ul>
        <li>Understanding Microservices Architecture</li>
        <li>What is Containerization and When is it Beneficial?</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

## Getting started

<div className="not-prose grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-6 my-6">
  <div className="rounded-lg border border-(--border) bg-(--card) overflow-hidden">
    <div className="px-5 py-3 bg-(--muted) border-b border-(--border)">

      <ul>
        <li>Concentrates on the basics of the product or service, how to set it up, and how to perform key tasks.</li>
        <li>Tends to be shorter, aiming for quick assimilation of basic information.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-lg border border-(--border) bg-(--card) overflow-hidden">
    <div className="px-5 py-3 bg-(--muted) border-b border-(--border)">

    </div>
    <div className="px-5 py-4 text-sm text-(--foreground)">
      <ul>
        <li>Used at the beginning of a user's journey.</li>
        <li>Aimed at product or service users, focusing on the functional aspects of how to use it.</li>
      </ul>
      <h5>EXAMPLES</h5>
      <ul>
        <li>Getting started with Zoom</li>
        <li>Getting Started with React</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<details>
<summary>What's the difference between a Getting Started and How-to topic?</summary>

Both serve distinct but complementary purposes. While both aim to assist users in understanding and using a product, service, or tool, they differ in scope, detail, and intended user.

#### Similarities

- Both are designed to provide step-by-step assistance, helping users accomplish specific tasks or understand product functionalities. They are usually sequential, guiding users through a logical order.
- Both focus on the user's perspective, presenting information clearly and easily digestibly.
- Both often incorporate visuals like screenshots, diagrams, or videos to enhance understanding.

#### Differences

<tr>
  <th width="50%">Getting Started</th>
  <th width="50%">How-to</th>
</tr>
<tr>
    <td><p>Focused on the <strong>basics of a specific product or tool</strong>. It's usually concise, covering only what is necessary to effectively use the product or tool.</p>
<p>It is often used for single sessions and is designed for quick reference and immediate application.</p></td>
    <td><p>Provides <strong>in-depth instructions on a specific scenario or goal.</strong> It's focused on detailed processes.</p>
<p>Designed for users who have basic knowledge but need guidance on specific functions.</p>
<p>Goes into detailed steps, often covering complex tasks or advanced features.</p>
<p>Can be lengthier and more complex, depending on the scenario&#39;s complexity.</p></td>

## Troubleshooting

<div className="not-prose grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-6 my-6">
  <div className="rounded-lg border border-(--border) bg-(--card) overflow-hidden">
    <div className="px-5 py-3 bg-(--muted) border-b border-(--border)">
      <h3 className="text-base font-semibold text-(--foreground) m-0">What is it</h3>
    </div>
    <div className="px-5 py-4 text-sm text-(--foreground)">
      <span className="inline-block px-2 py-0.5 text-xs font-semibold rounded bg-(--muted) text-(--muted-foreground) border border-(--border) mb-2">Recommended</span>
      <ul>
        <li>Focuses on diagnosing and resolving specific issues or errors encountered by users.</li>
        <li>Addresses multiple potential causes and solutions, often taking a non-linear approach to account for various variables that might lead to the issue.</li>
        <li>Guides the user through logical steps to identify the root cause and resolve the problem.</li>
        <li>Generally reactive and designed to handle user frustration or confusion when something isn't working as expected.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-lg border border-(--border) bg-(--card) overflow-hidden">
    <div className="px-5 py-3 bg-(--muted) border-b border-(--border)">
      <h3 className="text-base font-semibold text-(--foreground) m-0">When to use it</h3>
    </div>
    <div className="px-5 py-4 text-sm text-(--foreground)">
      <ul>
        <li>Users encounter unexpected issues or specific error messages while using your product or service.</li>
        <li>Users don't know where to start solving a problem and need straightforward, step-by-step guidance.</li>
        <li>Providing multiple paths to resolution, as problems often have more than one potential cause.</li>
        <li>Minimizing user frustration by addressing frequent or common errors quickly and effectively.</li>
      </ul>
      <h5>EXAMPLES</h5>
      <ul>
        <li>Resolving Version Mismatch Errors in Microservices</li>
        <li>Fixing Error Code 404 in API Requests</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>
