AI-generated content offers opportunities to streamline workflows and deliver documentation efficiently. Using AI tools effectively requires careful planning.

## Guidelines for using AI-generated content

When incorporating AI-generated content into technical documentation, maintaining high standards of quality and accuracy is essential.

This means:

- **Review thoroughly and adhere to standards:** Treat AI-generated content as a starting point, not the final product.
  - Check for duplicate content, as multiple sections might inadvertently cover the same topic, leading to confusion and redundancy.
  - Ensure content follows your style guide and accurately conveys technical concepts. Use title case for the page title and sentence case for headings to maintain consistent, professional documentation.
  - Ensure content is detailed and comprehensive. Avoid simple lists without explanations and examples to guide readers through the product or process.
  - Structure lists for easy scanning. Keep points direct and concise.
- **Complement, don't replace:** Use AI to augment human expertise, not to replace it. Use AI tools for efficiency, but rely on human judgment for clarity, tone, and nuance.
- **Be transparent:** Disclose when AI tools contribute to your content. Transparency builds trust and helps users understand how the content was created.

## Best practices for using AI in documentation

- **Automate repetitive tasks:** Use AI to handle routine tasks like generating boilerplate text or formatting. For example, AI can generate standard operating procedures (SOPs) by filling in templates with specific details, freeing human writers to focus on more complex work.
- **Train on quality data:** Ensure your AI models learn from accurate, reliable, and inclusive datasets to produce high-quality content.

  :::caution
  Few AI models are currently trained on high-quality datasets. Always review the output to ensure it aligns with your style guide and accurately conveys technical concepts.
  :::

- **Stay current:** Regularly review and update AI-generated content to ensure it remains accurate, relevant, and aligned with evolving standards.
- **Monitor for bias:** Check AI-generated content for bias and ethical concerns. Avoid gender stereotypes in technical examples, use inclusive language (such as "team members" rather than "guys"), and ensure documentation represents diverse scenarios and perspectives.

## Pitfalls of AI-generated content

AI is a powerful tool, but understanding its limitations is essential for producing accurate and ethical content. These are the most common challenges:

- **Lack of style guide adherence:** AI tools don't inherently understand or follow organizational style guides, creating inconsistencies in terminology, formatting, and voice.
- **Lack of contextual understanding:** AI struggles with nuance and context, which can result in misinterpretations or content that misses the intended meaning.
- **Training data bias:** Because AI learns from existing data, it can amplify biases in its training data, producing content that lacks diversity or perpetuates stereotypes.
- **Ethical concerns:** AI raises questions about transparency and fairness — who gets credit for the content, and how does it affect opportunities for human creators?
- **Formulaic output:** AI tends to produce predictable content that lacks the originality and depth that human writers bring.
